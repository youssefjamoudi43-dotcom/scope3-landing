Questions

What consultants ask before piloting.

Does our client data ever reach your servers?+
No. Scope3 is self-hosted — it runs entirely on your own infrastructure, including during the pilot. Documents are read, processed, and stored on your side only. We never see them.
What happens after the 3-day pilot?+
Nothing, automatically. The pilot license simply expires on its own. If it was useful, we talk about an annual license from there — no auto-renewal, no surprise charge, no obligation either way.
What if the extraction gets something wrong?+
That's what the verification and review-flag layers are for. Every figure is checked against its cited source page, and anything ambiguous — a target that could be mistaken for an actual, a unit that doesn't match — gets flagged for a person to review. Nothing is finalized without a human approving it.
Does this replace our review process?+
No — it speeds up the first pass, not the judgment call. Your team still approves, overrides, or rejects every metric before it's used. Scope3 removes the manual reading and copying, not the review.
Which CSRD/ESRS datapoints does it cover?+
22 datapoints across climate (Scope 1/2/3 emissions, energy, water, waste), workforce (turnover, training, pay gap, safety), and governance (board composition, ethics, corruption). That's not the full ESRS taxonomy — it's the set of metrics that come up most often in practice. Happy to talk through what's not covered before you pilot.
Do we need a technical team to run this?+
You need someone comfortable running a Docker container — not a dev team. Setup is a handful of commands, documented step by step, and we're happy to walk through it live for the pilot.
What does it cost after the pilot?+
An annual self-hosted license, priced to fit how many client engagements you run through it. We'd rather quote you a number based on what the pilot actually showed than guess at one cold — that conversation happens after, not before.
